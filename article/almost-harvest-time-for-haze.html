Almost Harvest time for Haze

As our cannabis cultivation journey progresses, it's essential to pause and evaluate the growth of our plants. Observing Haze, our robust Amnesia strain, we can see significant development in size and structure.

Haze was planted back on September 17th, 2023. We used a mix of Walmart and Amazon soil.

The key to understanding the perfect harvest time lies in the microscopic examination of trichomes. These minute, crystal-like formations on the bud surfaces hold the secret to maturity and potency.

A close inspection of these trichomes with a magnifying glass reveals much about the plant's status. Clear trichomes suggest that the harvest can wait, as THC levels have not yet peaked. When they adopt a cloudy white hue, it's an indicator of optimal THC concentration, signaling that harvest time is ideal. Trichomes with an amber color are often a sign of diminishing THC potency, indicating a later stage in the plant's lifecycle.

The pistils, too, give us a visual cue. Initially pale and straight, when they darken and curl, it's a visual confirmation of the plant's maturity.

Lastly, the buds themselves should feel dense and sticky to the touch, a tactile sign that they are ripe with resin and ready for harvest.

With that in mind, I took a small clipping from the plant.

A close inspection of these trichomes with a magnifying glass reveals much about the plant's status. While getting the microscope still and focused enough to take these pictures was difficult, it ended up being definitely worth it. As you can see, many of them are white and milky, I would say about 75-85% of them. We are definitely close!

We are going to give them one more week to mature before harvesting Haze! I look forward to seeing how much weight we get from her!
