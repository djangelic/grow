I recently realized that some of my stress with growing was not having the information I needed at hand. For example when I needed to harvest, among other things, so being a total nerd, I went ahead and created a spreadsheet. I clipped the notes section, but you get the idea.

From there, to be able to tie the plant itself to the data, we gave them names and labels.

Which made me feel so good, to see the garden all tagged properly:

With so many plants to care for, I need to get my automatic watering pump back up and running. To that end, I finally DIY'ed a solution to the hose cover that fits perfectly. I had previously put a water gallon lid on the water jug, but it didn't fit well and would fall off, encouraging algae growth. I finally used a sharp blade to cut out the sides of the water cap, and replaced it with the actual lid that came with the jug, but with the spout portion removed. It now looks like this:

One of the other things we've been dealing with as well is nutrient burn. I think I aggressively gave the plants too much nutrients and Haze developed a yellow splotchy color on the leaves. I've gone ahead and switched to only water to flush out the soil, and it seems to be working out great! Photo on the left is from today, and the right is 4 days ago:

Otherwise, the new seedlings are getting along great!
